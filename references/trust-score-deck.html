Challenges We Met

Security Scope Creep

Phase 3 initially included full login flows, password handling, refresh tokens, and user management for a product with one client.

→ Rolled back. JWT-only auth. Smaller attack surface, zero wasted complexity.

Real-World Data Is Messy

Walgreens' Excel had 3 missing fields, 2 out-of-range values, and 1 metric below schema minimum.

→ Built empathetic ingestion that guides rather than blocks. Every client's data will have quirks.

The Polish Problem

Score rings animated to mock values, then jittered when real API data arrived. The kind of thing a CMO notices.

→ Gating pattern: rings hold at zero until real data confirmed, then animate once.

Deployment Chain

Vercel ↔ Railway required solving CORS, env var injection timing, and framework auto-detection.

→ Each solvable, but each could have been a multi-day blocker for someone new.

Appendix
Methodology Deep Dive — Pillar Weight Rationale

Grounded in three bodies of research. All source reports available on file.

Accountability — 40%

Research basis: Edelman1 identifies "Reliable/Delivers on promises" as the #1 trust attribute globally (63% of respondents). McKinsey2 finds response speed and resolution rate are the two highest-leverage levers for service trust in consumer businesses.

Why 40% and not higher: Accountability without Authenticity produces compliance, not loyalty. A brand that always responds but has no authentic voice or community will plateau. The 40/30/30 split reflects the research hierarchy while preserving the other pillars as meaningful contributors.

Authenticity & Credibility — 30%

Research basis: Edelman's1 pandemic studies (2020–2024) established that brand trust increasingly depends on perceived honesty and values alignment. Qualtrics3 identifies sentiment-based metrics as leading indicators of loyalty — they predict churn before purchase behavior changes.

Limitation acknowledged: 30% may be conservative given AI-content skepticism acceleration (2024–2026). Weight to be re-evaluated after Phase 2 regression against Walgreens business outcome data.

Community — 30%

Research basis: Qualtrics3 shows advocacy-stage customers (NPS Promoters, active community members) generate 2.5–4× the lifetime value of passive customers. Community metrics are powerful but downstream — they form after Accountability and Authenticity are established.

Framework mapping: 30% reflects Community's importance as an outcome metric and lagging indicator. NPS placement validated by Reichheld4 framework. Phase 2 regression may adjust.

Appendix
Methodology Deep Dive — Sub-Category Weights & Metric Types
Accountability Sub-Categories
Dependability (55%): McKinsey2 identifies response speed and resolution as the highest-leverage trust levers in service businesses. Takes larger share because it reflects active trust-building behavior.

Fulfillment (45%): Ratings, reviews, critical mentions. Important but more of a hygiene factor — reflects product quality rather than active trust-building.
Authenticity Sub-Categories
Relatability (40%): Behavioral trust — engagement, sentiment, UGC. Qualtrics3 shows CX sentiment as a leading indicator. Largest sub because behavioral signals are the most direct trust proxy.

Congruence (35%): Two complementary metrics — Key Pillar Mention Rate (65%, the audience echo) and On-Brand Content Ratio (35%, the brand signal). Together they validate congruence from both sides.

Expertise (25%): Moved from Accountability per Edelman1 "Competence" framework. Leadership posting, SOV, search authority. Contributes 7.5% to total score (nearly doubled from prior 4%).
Community Sub-Categories
Belonging (50%): Community Footprint composite — Active Audience Ratio (60%), Follower Count (15%), Growth Rate (25%). AAR is the key signal: "what % of followers actually showed up?" Qualtrics3 finds engagement quality outperforms audience size as a predictor of retention.

Advocacy (50%): NPS4, fan accounts, influencer reach, employee participation. Edelman1 ranks employee voices as the most credible form of brand communication. Equal split with Belonging is the defensible baseline.
Standard Metrics

Always included. Required data. Missing = error. Core trust signals that every brand must report.

Bonus Metrics

Optional. Additive lift when present (e.g., Raves Rate). Capped so it can't distort. Absent = no penalty. Rewards brands that go above and beyond.

ConditionalStandard Metrics

When data exists → weighted normally. When absent → remaining metrics renormalize. Absence disclosed transparently. Prevents penalizing brands for not yet tracking a metric (e.g., Resolution Rate2, Employee Participation1).

Data Architecture — Where the 43 Metrics Come From

The engine scores data you hand it. All 43 metrics must exist as numbers in the uploaded file. Here's where each category of data originates.

Owned Social (8–10 metrics)
Engagement rates (FB/IG/TikTok), follower count, active audience ratio, audience growth rate, leadership posting, on-brand content ratio

Source: Platform analytics or scraping API
Automatable: Yes — Sprinklr, Apify, or platform-native APIs
Earned / Listening (12–15 metrics)
Critical mention rate, raves rate, comment sentiment, UGC rate, key pillar mention rate, leadership mentions, SOV

Source: Social listening tool
Requires: Sprinklr, Brandwatch, or Mention.com running for 30+ days
Reviews + Client-Provided (15–18 metrics)
Rating, reviews, NPS, resolution rate, employee participation, advocacy program data, influencer program data, search rank, ad conversion rates

Source: Client surveys, CRM, internal HR/comms
Key insight: These are the metrics that differentiate — and they require client partnership
The Honest Answer

A scraping API gets you ~8–10 of 43 metrics automatically. The rest requires a listening tool (Sprinklr, Brandwatch, Mention) or client-provided data. Sprinklr's value isn't just scraping — it's aggregated listening + sentiment + competitive monitoring in one place.

Client Onboarding — What It Takes to Run a Score
Tier 1 — Client Provides Directly
  • NPS score (current period average)
  • Resolution rate (% issues resolved)
  • Employee participation rate
  • Advocacy program exists (Y/N) + participation
  • Influencer program exists (Y/N)
  • Ad conversion rates by platform
  • L&D social support program (Y/N)
Tier 2 — We Pull With Their Access
  • Social metrics: follower count, engagement rates (read access to Business Suite / TikTok Analytics)
  • Review data: Google Business rating + count (public)
  • Leadership posting: exec LinkedIn/IG post counts (public)
Tier 3 — Needs Listening Tool (30-Day Lead)
  • Critical mention rate, raves rate
  • Comment net sentiment, UGC rate
  • Key pillar mention rate (requires defining brand pillars first)
  • Share of voice (requires competitor list)
  • Earned influencer reach & engagement
  • Community group participation signals
The 30-Day Lead Time

For Tier 3 metrics, you need historical listening data. For a first-time client, three options: (1) use industry benchmarks as proxies for the first period with disclosure, (2) start the listening tool 30 days before the first score, (3) define those metrics as "data not provided" — the engine handles them as ConditionalStandards.

The Selling Point

The engine's ability to handle missing ConditionalStandard metrics — scoring without penalizing, with full disclosure — means you can run a partial score on day one and fill in the gaps over time. The product meets the client where they are.
